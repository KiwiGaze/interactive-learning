# The Silk Road

From roughly 130 BCE to the 1450s, a network of overland routes linked China and the Mediterranean world. The name "Silk Road" was coined by Ferdinand von Richthofen in 1877.

## Origins

Han emperor Wu sent the envoy Zhang Qian west in 138 BCE to seek allies against the Xiongnu. He returned with information about Central Asian kingdoms; regular trade followed.

## Key cities

- Chang'an (modern Xi'an) — eastern terminus
- Samarkand — major oasis and transhipment hub
- Bukhara — scholarly and mercantile center
- Palmyra — desert caravan city on the Mediterranean approach
